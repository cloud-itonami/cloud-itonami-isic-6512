Non-life insurance

Open Business Blueprint for ISIC Rev.5 6512
ISIC: 6512 Domain: Property & casualty insurance Status: Public OSS

The Problem

LLMs can draft underwriting checklists and flag KYC gaps, but they have no license to bind policies, no understanding of jurisdiction-specific regulatory requirements, and no way to know if a settlement exceeds a policy's coverage limit. Letting an LLM decide coverage autonomously invites fabricated requirements, undetected sanctions violations, and silent liability.

The Solution

An independent Non-Life Insurance Governor — not AI magic, but a separate system that holds unsafe commits. It wraps the LLM in a supervisory layer that re-verifies every proposal against your actual policy records, enforces six hard checks, and escalates to your human underwriters for final binding and settlement. No policy is bound and no claim is settled without explicit human approval, by construction.

What's Covered

  • Policy intake and per-jurisdiction underwriting-document checklisting
  • Policyholder and claimant KYC-sanctions screening
  • Policy-binding proposal with spec-basis citations
  • Claim filing against bound policies
  • Claim-settlement proposal with coverage validation
  • Immutable audit ledger for all operations

What's Out of Scope

This actor does not hold a license to underwrite insurance in any jurisdiction. It does not handle premium billing, real banking integration, damage-site routing, or policy endorsements. Your licensed operations team supplies the jurisdiction-specific license, real KYC/AML, actual claims adjusters, and bears the regulatory liability. The Governor is the compliance scaffold that lets you focus on customers, not re-building guardrails for every market.

How It Works

Underwriter-LLM ⊣ Non-Life Insurance Governor: The LLM drafts proposals. The Governor independently verifies them against your records, enforces hard guardrails (sanctions hits, incomplete docs, coverage violations), and routes binding and settlement to your human operators for final approval.

Audit Ledger: Every intake, assessment, screening, binding, and settlement decision is immutably recorded. Your compliance team can prove who approved what, when, and on what basis.
