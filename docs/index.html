About

A forkable, governable business model for property and casualty insurance operations. This repository publishes an underwriting and claims-settlement actor that any qualified, licensed operator can fork, deploy, run and sell. Built on the langgraph-clj StateGraph runtime, this actor seals an Underwriter-LLM into a single node and wraps it with an independent Non-Life Insurance Governor, a human approval workflow, and an immutable audit ledger to ensure policies are never bound and claims are never settled without human sign-off.

No policy is bound and no claim is settled autonomously, at any phase, by construction. The governor enforces six hard checks: spec-basis citation, sanctions screening, document completeness, policy binding state, claim-to-policy binding, and coverage-limit validation. This ensures the actor drafts and proposes, but a human operator (a licensed underwriter or claims adjuster) is always the one who actually binds coverage or settles a claim.

Offer

  • Policy intake and per-jurisdiction underwriting-document checklisting
  • Policyholder and claimant KYC-sanctions screening
  • Policy-binding proposal with spec-basis citations
  • Claim filing against bound policies
  • Claim-settlement proposal with coverage validation
  • Immutable audit ledger for all operations

Architecture

Underwriter-LLM ⊣ Non-Life Insurance Governor: The Underwriter-LLM drafts intake checklists, normalizes policy data, flags KYC gaps, and proposes binding and settlement decisions. The Non-Life Insurance Governor independently verifies each proposal against the actor's own records and enforces hard guardrails before any real-world action. The actor may recommend, but a human operator always approves final binding or settlement.

Robotics: This blueprint is designed on the premise that a robot performs the physical domain work. A claims-site damage-inspection drone documents property and vehicle loss for the human adjuster under this actor's governance. High-stakes actions require human sign-off; the governor never dispatches hardware autonomously.

Trust Controls

  • No policy is bound and no claim is settled without human sign-off
  • Sanctions hits, incomplete documents, and coverage violations force a hold (not an override)
  • Every intake, assessment, KYC, binding, claim and settlement path is auditable
  • Emergency manual override paths remain outside LLM control
